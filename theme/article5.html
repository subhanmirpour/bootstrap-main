Manufacturing Automation Laboratory (MAL) at UBC

  • Research Focus
  • Manufacturing Automation

The Manufacturing Automation Laboratory (MAL) at the University of British Columbia (UBC) is a globally recognized center for research in machining and automation technology. The lab conducts cutting-edge research in areas such as micro-machining, CNC machine tools, intelligent machining, and spindle design. Their high-impact academic articles are among the most-cited in the field of manufacturing.

At MAL, advanced tools and facilities, including CNC machining centers, ultra-high-speed micro-machining equipment, and piezo actuators, enable them to develop industry-leading technologies. The lab works closely with over 200 leading companies and universities across North and South America, Asia, and Europe, collaborating on joint research projects and advancing the field of intelligent manufacturing.

For stakeholders and investors, the work at MAL is highly relevant as the lab focuses on developing practical solutions for real-world manufacturing challenges. With expertise in automation, precision machining, and sensor-assisted intelligent machining, MAL is helping drive the adoption of cutting-edge technologies that are essential for the future of industries like automotive, aerospace, and manufacturing at large.
